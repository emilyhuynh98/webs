about me
Hi, my name is Emily Huynh. I'm a student interested in the process of learning, doing, and making, all the way from the hardware to the software.
WHO I AM

I am an undergraduate currently majoring in bioengineering and electrical engineering and computer science (EECS) at the University of California, Berkeley as one of 200 Regent's and Chancellor's Scholars in the class of 2020. As a proactive student, I'm always looking for new ways to challenge myself and learn from others.

MY PASSION

I'm passionate about simplicity for what it entails. The beauty of simplicity is that it means ease-of-use and understanding not only to those who build, code, or write it, but to everyone who comes across it. I try to implement simplicity in everything I do, whether it's my solutions, explanations, or design to reach a base understanding with everyone I interact with.

MY MISSION

My goal is to increase accessibility to healthcare. Growing up in a low-income family, I learned to downplay my health issues to prevent any expensive trips to my doctor. Developing devices to diagnose and treat patients is a step towards challenging the healthcare infrastructure and empowering patients to put health first.

INTERESTING FACTS

I'm an avid Quora reader; their algorithm successfully zeroed in on my favorite topics: astronomy, physics, and politics. When I'm not studying or reading Quora, I enjoy going to the gym to relive my glory days in high school as a dedicated track athlete. In high school, I topped off my senior year track season as a CIF Southern Section long jump champion, my favorite memory to date.
